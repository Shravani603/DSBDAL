# 8. Visualize the data using Python libraries matplotlib, seaborn by plotting the graphs for airquality dataset.(Use Air_quality_forvisualization.csv)( Charts : Line chart, Barplot, Heatmap, Scatterplot, histogram, boxplot, violin, timeseries chart)

import pandas as pd
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import seaborn as sns

df = pd.read_csv("AQ_data.csv")   # or your file name
df.head()

df.shape

df.columns

# Line Chart

# Lineplot for first 30 records
plt.figure()
sns.lineplot(x=df.index[:30], y=df['so2'][:30], color='steelblue')
plt.title("SO2 Levels (First 30 Records)")
plt.xlabel("Index")
plt.ylabel("SO2")
plt.show()

- The line chart shows fluctuations in SO₂ levels across the first 30 observations, indicating varying air pollution levels.

# Bar Plot

#Average SO₂ by State
plt.figure()
sns.barplot(data=df, x='state', y='so2')
plt.xticks(rotation=90)
plt.title("Average SO2 by State")
plt.xlabel("State")
plt.ylabel("SO2")
plt.show()

- The bar plot compares average SO₂ levels across different states, showing that pollution levels vary from state to state


# Heatmap

# Correlation between numerical features.
plt.figure(figsize=(8,5))
corr = df.corr(numeric_only=True)
sns.heatmap(corr, annot=True)
plt.title("Correlation Heatmap")
plt.show()


- The heatmap shows correlations between pollutants, where RSPM and SPM have a strong positive relationship(0.8)


# Scatter Plot

# Relationship between NO2 vs PM2.5 (colored by type)
plt.figure()
sns.scatterplot(data=df, x='no2', y='pm2_5', hue='type')
plt.title("NO2 vs PM2.5")
plt.xlabel("NO2")
plt.ylabel("PM2.5")
plt.legend(title="Area type", bbox_to_anchor=(1, 1), loc='upper left')
plt.show()

- The scatter plot shows a positive relationship between NO₂ and PM2.5 concentrations across different area types


# Histogram

# Distribution of PM2.5
plt.figure(figsize=(8,6))
sns.histplot(df['pm2_5'], bins=30, kde=True, color="green")
plt.title("Histogram: PM2.5 Distribution")
plt.show()

- The histogram indicates that most PM2.5 values are concentrated in the lower range, with few extremely high values.


# Boxplot

plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))
sns.boxplot(x="type", y="no2", data=df)
plt.xticks(rotation=90)
plt.title("Box Plot: NO₂ Levels by Area Type")
plt.xlabel("Area Type")
plt.ylabel("NO₂ Concentration")
plt.show()

- The box plot shows the distribution of NO₂ levels across area types and highlights the presence of several outliers


# Violin Plot

plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))
sns.violinplot(x="type", y="no2", hue="type", data=df, inner="box", palette="muted", legend=False)
plt.xticks(rotation=90)
plt.title("Violin Plot: NO₂ Distribution by Area Type")
plt.xlabel("Area Type")
plt.ylabel("NO₂ Concentration")
plt.show()

- The violin plot displays the density and spread of NO₂ concentrations for different area types.


# Time Series Chart

df_small = df.iloc[:100]

plt.figure()
plt.plot(df_small.index, df_small['no2'], color='steelblue')
plt.title("NO2 Time Series (First 100 Records)")
plt.xlabel("Index")
plt.ylabel("NO2")
plt.show()

- The time series chart shows fluctuations in NO₂ levels over the first 100 observations without a consistent trend.

| Column Name                   | Full Form                               | Meaning                                                                                                |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| `stn_code`                    | Station Code                            | Unique ID given to an air quality monitoring station.                                                  |
| `sampling_date`               | Sampling Date                           | Date on which the air sample was collected.                                                            |
| `state`                       | State                                   | Name of the Indian state where monitoring was done.                                                    |
| `location`                    | Location                                | City or area where the monitoring station is situated.                                                 |
| `agency`                      | Monitoring Agency                       | Organization responsible for collecting air quality data.                                              |
| `type`                        | Area Type                               | Type of area such as Residential, Industrial, or Sensitive area.                                       |
| `so2`                         | Sulphur Dioxide                         | Amount of SO₂ gas in air. It is a harmful pollutant mainly released from industries and burning fuels. |
| `no2`                         | Nitrogen Dioxide                        | Amount of NO₂ gas in air. Mainly produced by vehicles and industrial emissions.                        |
| `rspm`                        | Respirable Suspended Particulate Matter | Fine dust particles (PM10) that can enter the lungs and affect breathing.                              |
| `spm`                         | Suspended Particulate Matter            | Total suspended dust particles present in air.                                                         |
| `location_monitoring_station` | Location of Monitoring Station          | Exact place/name of the station where pollution was measured.                                          |
| `pm2_5`                       | Particulate Matter 2.5                  | Very tiny particles (diameter ≤ 2.5 micrometers) harmful to lungs and heart.                           |
| `date`                        | Date                                    | General date column stored in date format for analysis.                                                |

### Important Pollutants

* **SO₂** → Causes breathing problems and acid rain.
* **NO₂** → Causes lung irritation and smog.
* **RSPM / PM10** → Dust particles harmful to respiratory system.
* **PM2.5** → Most dangerous because particles are extremely small and enter deep into lungs.

### Simple Difference

* **SPM** → All suspended particles in air.
* **RSPM (PM10)** → Smaller breathable particles.
* **PM2.5** → Ultra-fine particles, more harmful than PM10.
